1 Introduction

This section will provide a general introduction to this project, the functional requirements, its scope, and an overview of the SRS document as a whole.

1.1 General Description

“NuFu” is a subscription based media streaming service which charges a $10/monthly base fee for access to 10 show slots (access to ten series). Additional slots thereafter will come in at an additional $1.50 per slot and any movie can be rented for $0.50 for 24 hours. The passages below discuss the functions and details for the website which will serve as the platform for the “NuFu” streaming service.

1.3 Scope

This media streaming project is a web based subscription service designed to allow users to watch various movies, programs, and shows at a minimal cost. The user will be able buy a subscription to the service for an initial $10, which can be renewed every month.This initial cost grants them 10 series of their choosing for 30 days. If the user wishes for more shows, they can pay an additional $1.50 for a new series slot to watch for that month. If the subscribed user wishes to rent a movie for just 24 hours, they can pay $0.50 for just that. The video service will also have a comments section and the ability to rate the show on a basis of 1 to 5.

In addition to the media streaming service, this service also provides subscribed users with their own profile. This profile shows others the user's favorite shows and their general description (which can be modified by the user. The user is able to choose which series they wish to watch through the user of a search bar, which can find shows by name, genre, year, service, and studio. Additionally, notifications can be enabled by the user to let them know if a new episode or show has aired.

The entirety of this service will be done through the use of a cloud service and thus require Internet access by the user and the service itself.

2 Overview of Project

This section will provide a general overview of the project and its background.

2.1 Background

The first inspiration behind “NuFu” came after the constant hike in prices from streaming services. Most notably Netflix recently raised their prices to a whopping $12/month for the cheapest HD plan. This caused an uproar amongst many casual customers who have netflix just to watch a few select shows and movies. Although the $12 price tag might be worth it for what Netflix is offering in return, many users have and most likely will not take full advantage to justify the increase in price. 

Secondly, many homes now have both Netflix and Hulu accounts to make sure they can watch shows from both platforms. However, that also means the user has to pay a premium $12/month for Netflix and an additional $10/month for Hulu. That is over $22/month just to watch a handful of shows for most people. This doesn’t include the additional $100+ most families pay for cable and the like. It has come to a point to where most teenagers and young adults have decided to either unsubscribe from these services completely or share an account with a friend to split the pricey costs. Netflix and other services have responded to account sharing between non-family members by enforcing a limit to how many people can watch simultaneously. However, this did not push the people account sharing to subscribe with their own account, rather it just made them stop using the service completely. All in all, people are paying for too much content, 99% of which they will never use nor do they have the time to make use of them. 

Finally, Disney announced last year that they will be coming out with their own streaming service. Following this announcement, they asked Netflix to discontinue shows that Disney has the licence to, most notably the Marvel show series. Most likely, Disney will continue those shows on their own service when it comes out in the near future but questions regarding who will play the characters the audience have grown to love and where will the show pick up from have had some customers outraged. This will just lead to another competing streaming service that most customers will likely have to subscribe to since Disney has the rights to a lot shows and movies.

2.3 Contextual Information

This service is modeled after similar streaming and video viewing sites. The primary site that this service is influenced from is Netflix with its subscription service and media viewing cite. Our project also gathers influence from websites like Youtube and various movie rating sites. Like Youtube, our service will allow users to rate videos, but instead of rating on the basis of "thumbs up" or "thumbs down," each user is allowed to rate on a scale of one star to five stars, with the comments being used to explain their reasoning. 

In contrast to Netflix, our service gives the user ten "slots," from which they pick certain series that they want to watch. Unlike sites like Amazon or Netflix, where the user has to pay the full subscription price even if they only use a small fraction of the services, our site is more affordable and allows the user to pick the shows that they actually want to see. This allows the user to pick and choose what they want to watch without wasting money on paying for services that they won't even use. The user is also able to buy a new slot for a new show for the month if they want to watch another one.

7 Logging, Security, and more

This section will cover the basics behind our plans for security and authentication.

7.1 Security

Our customers privacy is our concern so we pledge to create an infrastructure that will help disguise and hide our subscriber’s personal information. 

One way of going about ensuring privacy on our site will be by using encryption. There will be many encryption options available to us as we get closer to deploying this feature on our site. Currently we have decided to make it a priority to encrypt the user’s passwords, address, and credit card information. If need be later on, we might also encrypt the user’s full name to ensure their anonymity. 

Another feature we will implement to ensure the user’s security will be not to take information we do not need. It may seem obvious but many times a website or service will ask for information they never needed to have in the first place and this would just lead to more data being leaked if the service is compromised. For example, we will not be asking the user to access their microphones and cameras since our site has no use for these.
